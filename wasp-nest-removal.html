How to Locate a Wasp Nest

Finding a wasp nest early can help prevent stings and property damage. Here are some tips to locate nests safely:

  • Observe Wasp Activity: Watch where wasps are flying to and from. They usually travel in a straight line from the nest.
  • Check Common Nesting Sites: Nests are often found in lofts, eaves, wall cavities, garages, sheds, attics, and under roof tiles.
  • Look for Nest Materials: Wasp nests are made of a papery substance and are usually grey or brown. Large, visible nests may be attached to beams or branches.
  • Time of Day Matters: Wasps are most active in warm daylight hours, so morning or early afternoon observations are best.
  • Safety First: Never try to remove a nest yourself if it is large or difficult to reach. Disturbing a nest can lead to aggressive stings. Always call a professional.
